Instructions

In this study, you are playing in a group of size {{subsession.PLAYERS}}, and you will make a series of {{subsession.ROUNDS}} decision choices. Your decision will be to choose one of three strategies: A, B, or C. Each strategy will yield a different payoff for you which will depend on the strategies played by the other players in the game. After {{subsession.ROUNDS}} rounds, the game will end and the computer will randomly select 2 rounds. Your payoffs in those two randomly selected rounds will be added together and divided by {{subsession.OPPONENTS}}. That ammount, rounded to the nearest dollar, will be added to your show up fee of $5 and together will be your total payment for the study. Because payoff rounds are randomly selected, every decision you make has a chance of directly impacting your payment.

How Strategies are Selected

During each round you will be able to make or update your decision choice by clicking one of the three purple buttons corresponing to the three decisions available to select from (see table below). Your decision choice and its row in your payoff table will be highlighted yellow to help you keep track of what decision you have currently selected. At the end of every round, the computer will randomly (with probability = {{subsession.UPDATE}}) select to update your strategy from last round with your selected choice from this round. If your strategy fails to update, the computer will ignore your choice from this round and keep your strategy from your last round of play. If you do not make a choice during a given round, your strategy last round will be selected as your choice for this round. Your strategy for this round will be highlighted green at the start of the next round. Note: Everyone will start the game with A being their "last round strategy".

How Payoffs are Calculated

Payoffs for each round will be calculated based on your strategy (not choice, see above) and the strategies (not choices, see above) of all the other players in the game. Each round you get payoffs by pairing up with every player in the game. Your payoff in a pairing is determined by your strategy which determines the row of the payoff table, and the other player's strategy which determines the column of the payoff table, together selecting an individual cell in the payoff table. The red value in each cell is your payoff if that cell is selected{{ if subsession.INFORMATION }}, and the blue value is the payoff the other player gets{{ endif }}. The sum of the payoffs you get from every player in the game is your total payoff for a round.

For example, say there are 3 other players in the game.

If for a round your strategy is A and the three other players strategies are C then your payoff for that round will be: 0*{{subsession.PAYOFF_A}} + 0*{{subsession.PAYOFF_B}} + 3*{{subsession.PAYOFF_C}} = {{subsession.EXA}}.

If your strategy is B and the strategies of the other players are: 1 A, 0 B, 2 C. Then your payoff for that round will be: 1*{{subsession.PAYOFF_D}} + 0*{{subsession.PAYOFF_E}} + 2*{{subsession.PAYOFF_F}} = {{subsession.EXB}}.

If your strategy was instead C and the strategies of the other players are: 1 A, 0 B, 2 C. Then your payoff for that round would be: 1*{{subsession.PAYOFF_G}} + 0*{{subsession.PAYOFF_H}} + 2*{{subsession.PAYOFF_I}} = {{subsession.EXC}}.

How Rounds Progress

Each round lasts a fixed amount of time. Starting with 30 seconds for the first round, 20 seconds for rounds 2-9, and 10 seconds for rounds 11-{{subsession.ROUNDS}}. After each round, strategies and payoffs for all players are calculated. The information about your decision choice and strategy selected last round, the strategies of the other players, and your payoff from last round are automatically reported at the start of every new round. New rounds will automatically start as soon as the previous round finishes.
